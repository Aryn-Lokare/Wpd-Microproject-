The F1 rules that every fan needs to know

1. Qualifying Form : Formula 1 qualifying consists of three sessions (Q1, Q2, Q3), with each session eliminating the slowest cars until a final shootout determines the grid order.


>2.Grid Penalties: Drivers can receive grid penalties for various infractions, such as exceeding their engine allocation or causing avoidable accidents. These penalties push them down the grid order.


3.Points System: Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers in each race, with the winner receiving 25 points, followed by 18, 15, 12, 10, 8, 6, 4, 2, and 1 point(s) for the subsequent positions.


4. DRS (Drag Reduction System):DRS allows drivers to open a flap on their rear wing to reduce drag and increase straight-line speed. It can only be used in designated DRS zones when a driver is within one second of the car ahead.


5. Formation Lap: Before the race starts, drivers complete a formation lap to warm up their tires and brakes. They must maintain their positions relative to each other until reaching the starting grid.


6. Race Start: The race begins with a standing start or, in some cases, a rolling start behind the safety car in wet conditions. Drivers must adhere to strict rules regarding positioning and behavior at the start.


7.Blue Flags: When a driver is about to be lapped by a faster car, blue flags are shown to inform them to yield and allow the faster car to pass safely.


8. Track Limits: Drivers must stay within the track limits defined by white lines. Exceeding these limits can result in time penalties or disqualification if done repeatedly.


9. Safety Car: Deployed when there is a significant incident or hazardous conditions on the track. All cars must follow the safety car at reduced speed and can't overtake until it returns to the pits.


10. Virtual Safety Car (VSC): Similar to the safety car, but instead of deploying a physical car on track, drivers must maintain a specified lap time. Overtaking is prohibited during VSC periods.


11. Minimum Weight: Each car must weigh at least a specified minimum weight, including the driver and all onboard systems. Cars are weighed before and after the race to ensure compliance.


12. Parc Fermé: After qualifying and the race, cars are taken to parc fermé conditions where no significant changes can be made, except under strict guidelines from race stewards.


13. Flags: Various flags are used to communicate with drivers during the race, including yellow flags for caution, a red flag to stop the race, and a black flag for disqualification.


14. Pit Stops: Teams can make pit stops during the race to change tires, make adjustments, or address mechanical issues. Pit stops must adhere to strict safety and speed limits.


15. Drive-Through Penalty: A penalty where a driver must enter the pit lane and drive through it at the prescribed speed without stopping. It is usually imposed for minor infractions.


16. Chequered Flag: Indicates the end of the race. The race winner crosses the finish line when the chequered flag is waved, signaling the conclusion of the Grand Prix.
