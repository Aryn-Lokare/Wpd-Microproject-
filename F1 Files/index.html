History : -

In 1946, the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) was established to govern international motorsport. In 1950, the first Formula One World Championship race was held at Silverstone in the United Kingdom. Italian driver Giuseppe Farina won the inaugural championship driving for Alfa Romeo.The 1960s and 1970s saw significant advancements in technology and safety measures, as well as the emergence of iconic drivers like Juan Manuel Fangio, Jim Clark, Graham Hill, and Jackie Stewart. Manufacturers such as Ferrari, Lotus, and McLaren became prominent figures in the sport.The 1980s marked the introduction of turbocharged engines, leading to a period of intense rivalry between manufacturers such as Ferrari, McLaren, and Williams. Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, and Nelson Piquet emerged as dominant forces on the track.he 1990s and 2000s witnessed further technological advancements, including the introduction of semi-automatic gearboxes, traction control, and advanced aerodynamics. Michael Schumacher dominated this era, winning seven World Championships with Benetton and FerrariFormula 1 expanded its reach globally, hosting races in new markets such as China, Bahrain, and Abu Dhabi. The sport also underwent significant commercialization and modernization under the ownership of Bernie Ecclestone.In 2014, Formula 1 transitioned to hybrid power units, combining internal combustion engines with energy recovery systems. Mercedes-Benz emerged as the dominant team, winning multiple Constructors' and Drivers' Championships with Lewis Hamilton behind the wheel.

Upcoming Events

BAHRAIN GRAND PRIX

The Bahrain Grand Prix is a popular Formula One race held annually in Bahrain. The race takes place at the Bahrain International Circuit, which is located in the Sakhir desert, near Manama, Bahrain's capital city. The event attracts millions of spectators and is one of the most high-profile races on the F1 calendar. The first Bahrain Grand Prix was held in 2004 and has been a regular fixture ever since.


SAUDI ARABIAN GRAND PRIX

The Saudi Arabian Grand Prix (Saudi GP) is a racing event scheduled to take place in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia in 2024. It is set to be a part of the Formula One World Championship, marking the country's first Formula One race. The Saudi GP is organized by the Saudi Arabian Motorsport Company (SAMC) and will be held on a custom-built Formula One track. The race is expected to attract a large number of fans, and there will be various spectator zones and facilities available for visitors.


JAPANEESE GRAND PRIX

The Japanese Grand Prix (GP) is an annual motor racing event that takes place in Japan. The dates for the 2024 Japanese GP have not been confirmed yet. The event is typically held in the SUGO Speedway in Fuji City, Japan, and is a round of the Formula One World Championship. The Japanese GP is a popular event that attracts a large number of spectators and is one of the most historic and iconic races in the Formula One calendar.


AUSTRALIAN GRAND PRIX

The Australian Grand Prix is one of the longest running and most prestigious motorsports events in the world. It takes place annually in Melbourne, Australia, and is a key event on the Formula One racing calendar. The 2024 Australian Grand Prix is currently slated to take place on April 8-10. While further details about the specific dates and location of the race have not been released, it is expected to continue attracting a diverse range of spectators from around the world.


BRITAIN GRAND PRIX

The British Grand Prix is a race held in the United Kingdom as part of the Formula One World Championship. It is typically held at Silverstone Circuit in Northamptonshire and is considered one of the most important races on the F1 calendar. The 2024 British Grand Prix is still a few years away, but it is certain to be a highly anticipated event.
